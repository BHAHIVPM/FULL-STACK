INDIAN MILITARY FORCE

The Indian military consists of the Army, Navy, and Air Force, responsible for national defense. The Army is among the largest in the world, while the Navy secures maritime interests, and the Air Force handles aerial operations. It includes paramilitary forces for internal security and maintains nuclear capabilities. Modernization efforts focus on indigenization through initiatives like "Make in India."

ARMY

The Indian Army, established in 1895, is one of the largest standing armies in the world, dedicated to defending India’s sovereignty and territorial integrity. It comprises various regiments and corps, including infantry, artillery, and armored units, and operates under the motto "Service Before Self." The President of India is the Supreme Commander, while the Chief of Army Staff is the highest-ranking officer. The army plays a vital role in UN peacekeeping missions and disaster relief efforts. Rigorous training is conducted at institutions like the Indian Military Academy, with a growing inclusion of women in various roles. Modernization initiatives focus on advanced technology and joint exercises with other nations. Community engagement through education and health initiatives is a priority. Bravery is honored through awards like the Param Vir Chakra. The army actively participates in counter-terrorism operations and collaborates with other branches for national security. It remains a symbol of national pride and resilience.

RANKING

World 4th Rank in Military

The Indian military is one of the largest in the world, ranking among the top five global military powers. It has over a million active personnel and a significant defense budget, typically around $70 billion. India is also a recognized nuclear power, enhancing its strategic deterrence capabilities.

World's Third Air Force

The Indian Air Force (IAF) is ranked third in the world for air power, according to the World Directory of Modern Military Aircraft (WDMMA).

World's Eighth Navy

As of 2024, the Indian Navy is ranked 8th in the world in terms of the number of warships, with 295 ships. The Indian Navy's primary role is to protect the country's coastlines and islands.
